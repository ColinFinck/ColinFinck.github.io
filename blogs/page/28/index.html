Newsletter 7

Welcome to Issue 7 of the ReactOS Weekly Newsletter, as persistant as a Jehovah's Witness. This week, I'll be taking a look at the NDK used by the ReactOS Developers, covering WINE 0.9.2, and detailing the latest SVN activity. NDK: What it does, and why it's needed "What is the NDK designed to do?", I hear you ask. Well, first off, a little bit of it's history. The NDK stands for Native Development Kit, and was the brainchild of Alex Ionescu, as a way of allowing Windows and ReactOS developers alike to have access to a wealth of undocumented kernel and native structures and function prototype.

ReactOS Weekly Newsletter Volume 1, Issue 5 The Number keeps creeping up, and I believe, with this Issue, the ReactOS Weekly Newsletter equals the amount of editions Splash, the former ReactOS Newsletter, released Freeloader: What is it? Freeloader (abbreviated freeldr [Why is abbreviated such a long word? -Ed.]), is the ReactOS Bootloader, licensed under the GPL. What does it do? It parses the ReactOS registry, and examines particular keys for certain values to act upon.
